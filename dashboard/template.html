What does “97% graphitized” actually mean?

X-ray diffraction's Degree of Graphitization (DG%) is computed from where the carbon (002) peak sits — not how much of the sample actually got there. A scan can read DG ≈ 97% while a large mass fraction is still amorphous, because the disordered carbon scatters as a broad hump with no sharp peak to average in. The panels below ground that gap in the 17 real furnace runs on file, then let you drive the mass-balance / kinetics model that tries to explain it.

4 · What backs each claim

Every mechanism in the model is grounded in the literature (full list + DOIs in SOURCES.md). The key ones:
Process & Fe catalysis
  • Banavath et al., npj Materials Sustainability 4:23 (2026) — the ≤1600 °C Fe-catalyzed route; Fe plateau ~50 wt%.
  • AIChE 2025 (paper 693e) — the fuel-grade (high-sulfur) extension.
  • Fe-catalyzed graphitization of isotropic carbon, Carbon (1983) — dissolution–reprecipitation via Fe carbide.
H1 sulfur trapping · H3 calcium (focus)
  • Poisoning of iron catalyst by sulfur, Appl. Catal. A (2007) — H₂S retards graphite growth on Fe (H1).
  • Ca-catalyzed graphenization, Nature Sci. Rep. (2023) — calcium co-catalyzes graphitization via a CaC₂ carbide route above ~1200 °C (H3).
  • CaO confinement / TOF-SIMS wetting (2018/2022) — calcium reduces Fe particle size, prevents sintering, and wets the Fe surface (H3).
Scale-up: kiln hold, heat transfer, industry
  • Kintek kiln-zone refs — the kiln heats, holds at peak (hot-zone traverse), then cools; the isothermal hold is real.
  • Pet-coke thermo-physical, Springer (2016) — raw coke k ≈ 2–4 W/m·K, so a thin cross-section heats/cools in under a minute; a thick one lags (L²/α).
  • Continuous graphitization furnaces (industry) — how the field scales (24 h, to 10,000 t/yr) vs batch Acheson (3000 °C); microwave/plasma emerging.

Kinetics fit against 17 real XRD scans + 6 weighed mass-balance rows (least squares, RMSE noted above). The tube-furnace program reproduces the group's actual C01–T10 controller schedule (preheat holds at 300/800/1200 °C, process hold, programmed + natural cooling); the rotary kiln is a fast-entry → isothermal hold → fast-cool hot-zone profile. A lumped-capacitance material temperature lags the gas by a thermal time constant set by the piece's cross-section (not its total mass), so a thin lab puck tracks the furnace while a thick bed lags — and an extruder, holding the cross-section constant, scales throughput without a heat-transfer penalty. The in-browser simulator re-integrates the same ODE system with a fixed-step RK4, verified numerically identical to the reference LSODA solver across the temperature / CaCO₃ / reactor / geometry / binding / O₂ grid, so every control runs client-side. Scale-up levers are predictive (literature-grounded mechanisms; see §4 and SOURCES.md) with no scale-up data to fit yet. Real recipe compositions are shown because this page is private to your lab.
